Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago is the Philippines' oldest Hispanic stone fortification, and its beautifully manicured grounds on the Pasig River allow for a nice respite from the hustle and bustle of the metropolis. It was used as a storage facility for munitions by the SpaniardsÂ before being turned into a jail cell for activists and political dissidents during the Japanese occupation. National hero Jose Rizal was imprisoned at the fort before his execution in 1896, and it has now been transformed into a museum commemorating Rizal's life, his tragic death at the hands of the Spanish, and the ripple effects of his sacrifice on the Philippine cause for independence. It also features a garden square named Plaza Moriones and a military barracks that is now a central plaza called Plaza de Armas.
