Let us learn first about the history behind the Walled City of the Philippines

The walled city of Intramuros was founded from 1571 to 1898 during Spanish rule. From the name itself, Intramuros means "inside the wall", encompassing what was then the entire city of Manila. The city served as the administrative center of the Spanish occupation, sheltering Spanish government officials and their families, basically, the city was where the most influential and wealthy citizens of colonial Manila lived. The walls were six meters high and three kilometers long, and they surrounded a 160-acre area, which was built to protect the city against foreign invasions. It only has seven defensive gates were the only way in and out. Churches, hospitals, government offices, military barracks, schools, and mansions of the Spanish nobility were all located inside the city's 51 blocks.
